How it works

How FlashLingo works

01

Import a deck

Choose an official .flashjp deck or restore a user progress backup. FlashLingo verifies the package, reads its manifest, imports the database and prepares the deck so you can start studying and building vocabulary.

02

Study your cards

Practice recognition cards to understand words and production cards to actively recall them. Cards can include definitions, examples, translations, readings, audio, images and additional forms.

03

Review with spaced repetition

FlashLingo schedules your reviews based on your performance thanks to our algorithm. New, learning, review and relearning cards are organized within a daily study queue.

04

Analyze and improve

Use deck statistics to review your accuracy, study time, difficult cards, review history and upcoming load. Export your data whenever you need.

Main features

Everything you need to study vocabulary with focus.

.flashjp deck packages

Import FlashLingo packages that can contain a SQLite database, metadata, audio, images and recommended deck settings.

Recognition and production

Each word can generate two study directions: recognizing the word in the target language and producing it from its meaning.

Write mode

On production cards, write mode lets you type your answer, compare it with the target word and unlock Good only when you reach the configured minimum percentage.

Advanced settings

Each deck can have its own daily limits, learning steps, lapse behavior, algorithm parameters, mixing, reminders, write mode and undo.

Detailed statistics

Check accuracy, study time, review forecast, daily activity, difficult cards and session history through clear charts and summaries.

CSV, PDF and backups

Export study statistics as CSV or PDF, and create manual .flashjp backups of each deck's progress whenever you want to keep or move your data.

Local reminders

Enable optional per-deck reminders. FlashLingo uses local notifications to remind you when cards are waiting for review.

Multilingual interface

FlashLingo supports an interface in English, Spanish, Romanian, German, French, Japanese and Chinese.

Privacy / Local-first

Your study progress belongs to you.

FlashLingo is designed as a local-first app. You don't need to create an account, and FlashLingo doesn't use its own server to store your decks or progress.

Your imported decks, media files, study history, deck settings, sessions and statistics are stored in the app's local storage on your device.

Read the Privacy Policy
  • You don't need to create an account.
  • No first-party analytics or first-party crash-reporting server.
  • Study progress is stored locally.
  • Deck backups are created only when you choose to export them.
  • Ads are provided by Google AdMob on compatible mobile builds.
  • Ad privacy options are handled through Google's consent flow when required.

For deck creators

Create your own decks for FlashLingo.

FlashLingo is built around portable deck packages. A .flashjp package can contain a database, a manifest, audio files, images, deck icons and settings values.

This lets you create richer vocabulary decks, with examples, translations, readings, multimedia and custom study settings.

And not only that: you can create decks for any topic or purpose, because the .flashjp format is very flexible.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to create an account?

No. FlashLingo doesn't require an account to study. Your decks and progress are stored locally on your device.

Does FlashLingo sync my progress to the cloud?

No. The current version doesn't include cloud sync. You can manually export a deck's progress as a .flashjp backup.

What is a .flashjp file?

A .flashjp file is a FlashLingo package used to import official decks or restore user progress backups.

Can I study offline?

You can study the decks you already imported locally. Some features, like ads or opening external links, may require an internet connection.

Does the app contain ads?

Yes, but they are non-intrusive and don't affect studying. FlashLingo may show ads through Google AdMob on compatible mobile builds.

Can I export my progress?

Yes. You can export statistics as CSV/PDF and create a manual .flashjp backup for each deck.
